How make a tortilla pizza.

How-to


1. Gather up your ingredients.
   Two tortillas per pizza.
   Cheese
   Salami/Pepperoni/Chorizo/Whatever meat you want.
   Ketchup or some other sauce.
   Spices if you want to make it more exciting.

2. Place a tortilla on your work surface.




3. Place cheese on the tortilla.
You don't need to use shredded cheese for this, sliced cheese works just as well. I just used what I had at hand.




4. Put your chorizos on the cheese.
You don't need to use chorizo for this. You can use leftovers from taco night, pepperoni, ham, cooked chicken, or whatever kind of filling you can get your hands on.




5. Put the sauce on your chorizos.



Again, use whatever sauce you want. I had a bottle of spicy ketchup in the cupboard, so I used that.




6. Add spices if you want.
I added some oregano and a bit of a spice mix consisting of garlic, chilli, salt and probably a bunch of other stuff.




7. Add some more cheese on the top.




8. Put the top tortilla on.




9. Put your tortilla pizza in a frying pan on medium-low heat.




10. Make sure you don't burn your tortilla.




11. Fry it to the right consistency.
I like my tortillas kinda crispy but at the same time not so crispy that they snap.




12. Put that pizza on a plate.




13. The cheese should be melted all the way through.




14. Slice that pizza up.




15. Enjoy the fruits of your labour.


Info about this how-to

You can mouse over the images to resize them.

This is just an easy recipe for some cheap, tasty pizza-like circles. I believe my dad invented this, but he may have "stolen" the idea from someone else.

This is probably an extremely unhealthy meal unless you replace some of the ingredients with veggies or something.
I'm not a nutritionist, but if you want to maintain your figure without hours of exercise, you should stay away from this recipe.
